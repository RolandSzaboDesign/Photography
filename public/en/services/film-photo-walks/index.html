Why Analog Portraits?

In a world consumed by digital photography, film portraits stand out with their genuine, unfiltered beauty. It's a departure from the pixel-perfect, filtered shots that flood our feeds. Instead, film photography captures moments with an authentic depth and character that simply can't be replicated in digital.

  • Authenticity – Each frame is carefully crafted, embracing imperfections and real moments that tell your one-of-a-kind story. With its soft tones, rich textures, and subtle grain, film gives your images a timeless allure that you won't find in digital photos.
  • Timeless Elegance – Film portraits have a classic quality that transcends fleeting trends. Whether you prefer the timeless elegance of black-and-white or the vibrant colors of a sunny day, film preserves the essence of your personality, creating portraits that never go out of style.
  • A Mindful Connection – With film photography, every shot is deliberate, allowing for a deeper connection between you and your photographer. This mindful approach creates an atmosphere of trust, creativity, and genuine expressions.
  • Embrace Surprises – Unlike digital photography's instant feedback, film takes you on an exciting journey of anticipation. The anticipation of seeing the final results adds an element of wonder and joy to the process.
  • Tangible Memories – Holding a print in your hands brings back a sensory experience that scrolling through a screen simply can't match. Film portraits add a touch of magic to your living spaces, reminding you of the moments that truly matter.

You see, film photography isn't just about snapping pictures—it's about embarking on a journey. From selecting the perfect film stocks to calibrating old film cameras with care, every step is infused with anticipation and craftsmanship. It's a slower process, but trust me, the end result is worth it. The joy of waiting, unveiling your photos, and holding prints in your hands—it's an experience like no other.

The Process of Analog Portraits

With Film Portraits, I'll guide you through all the technical aspects of film photography, ensuring a seamless and enjoyable experience from start to finish.

  1. Personalized Film Selection – Choosing the right film stock can make a world of difference in the final outcome of your portraits. My expertise allows me to recommend the perfect film that complements your desired style, whether it's the timeless elegance of black-and-white or the vibrant hues of color film.
  2. A Collaborative Photoshoot – Your film portrait session is a collaborative journey where we work together to capture the essence of who you are. I'll create a relaxed and comfortable atmosphere, encouraging genuine expressions and natural interactions.
  3. Assistance with Development – Once your photoshoot is complete, we take care of the entire development process. We choose the best development service in town to handle your precious photos with the utmost care and get the most stunning results possible.
  4. Preserve Your Memories – We believe that holding a physical print in your hands brings a special connection to your memories. You have the option to have your beautiful photos printed, right from the lab, allowing you to relive those beautiful moments time and time again.

Frequently Asked Questions, Analog Edition

The following questions are specifically applicable to film portraiture photoshoots. If you haven't done so already, please read my Portraiture FAQ before continuing here.

How much does it all cost?
For a limited time, I'm offering my film photography services at material cost. This mean you only need to pay for the film stock, the development and scanning process, and the oprional printing costs. A more simple, black-and-white film with development and small size scan will cost around 6.000 HUF, but a more exotic color film with development and medium size scan with prints won't go over 20.000 HUF.
Can you provide guidance in choosing the right film stocks?
Absolutely! We understand that navigating the world of film stocks can be overwhelming, especially with the scarcity of certain options. If your preferred film stock has been discontinued or is temporarily out of stock, we will work closely with you to find an alternative that closely aligns with your vision.
How long does it take to receive the final images?
The turnaround time for receiving your final prints depends on the lab we choose for developing the film stock. The lab's processing time may vary, but rest assured that we prioritize quality and timely delivery.
Can I combine film and digital photography for my session?
Certainly! I understand that both film and digital photography have their unique advantages. If you prefer a blend of the two mediums, I can absolutely accommodate that request. We will discuss your vision and work together to create a seamless fusion of film and digital elements. For the digital images, my regular pricing applies.
What camera and lenses do you use for film portraits?
I use my trust old soviet-made Zenit E analog SLR camera for film photography with a variety of M42 mount lenses. I offer a wide variety of ~50mm lenses, but I sport a 135mm one with excellent sharpness. My favorite lens is the mightly Helios 44-2 with its swirly bokeh rendering. Definitely write me a message if you want to learn more about the lenses I have.
