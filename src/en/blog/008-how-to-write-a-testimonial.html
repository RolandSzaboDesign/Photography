As a portrait photographer in Budapest, I know how intimidating standing in front of a camera can feel. Helping people overcome that uncertainty is part of my work, and your review can be extremely valuable to somebody who is still deciding whether to book a photo shoot.

A thoughtful testimonial can help a potential client understand far more than whether you liked the finished photographs. It can show them what the experience felt like, how much guidance they received and whether their initial concerns were handled well.

Here are a few ways to write a review that reflects your own experience while also helping somebody else choose the right photographer with greater confidence.

Why your review matters

Your testimonial is more than a simple piece of feedback. A specific and honest review can help somebody understand what working with the photographer is genuinely like.

  • It builds trust: Honest reviews from genuine clients make a service more credible.
  • It describes the experience: A detailed testimonial shows future clients what may happen before, during and after the shoot.
  • It answers practical concerns: Your personal perspective may answer a question or worry shared by somebody considering a similar session.

What makes a useful photography review?

You do not need to include every point below. Choose the parts that genuinely mattered during your own experience.

1. Your initial concerns and expectations

Begin by describing how you felt before the session. Were you nervous? Did you dislike being photographed? Were you unsure how to pose or worried that the final images would not feel like you?

This gives readers a clear starting point and helps people with similar concerns recognise themselves in your experience.

I had never enjoyed being photographed before, and I was quite nervous about the session.

2. The photographer’s approach and personality

Describe how the photographer treated you and what helped you become more comfortable. Specific examples are generally more useful than simply saying somebody was friendly or professional.

Roland’s clear directions and relaxed sense of humour helped me settle in almost immediately.

3. What the photo shoot felt like

Write about the atmosphere and any part of the process that stood out. Did you receive useful guidance? Did the photographer listen to your ideas? Was there enough time to relax? Did anything happen that made the session particularly enjoyable or memorable?

Despite the cold weather, I enjoyed the complete outdoor session. Roland worked quickly and carefully, while still making the experience feel relaxed.

4. The finished photographs and your reaction

Explain what you thought when you first saw the photographs. Did they match your expectations? Did you recognise yourself in them? Are you using them on a website, professional profile, dating app or simply keeping them as a personal memory?

The finished photographs exceeded my expectations. For the first time, I genuinely liked how I looked in pictures.

5. A clear recommendation

Finish by explaining whether you would recommend the photographer and who might particularly benefit from working with them.

I would wholeheartedly recommend Roland, especially to people who normally feel uncertain in front of the camera. He knows how to bring out the best in people without making the experience feel forced.

A simple review template

You can use the following structure as a starting point. Rewrite it in your own words and remove anything that does not apply to your experience.

Before the photo shoot, I was [describe your concern or expectation].

Roland’s [mention his approach or something he did] helped me [describe how you felt during the session].

One part I particularly remember was [describe a specific moment].

When I saw the finished photographs, I [describe your reaction and how the images compared with your expectations].

Overall, working with Roland was [summarise the experience].

I would recommend him to [explain who you would recommend him to and why].

Final thoughts

Writing a persuasive review does not need to be difficult. By sharing your genuine experience and including a few specific details, you can help somebody else make a more confident decision.

Your words can explain parts of the experience that a portfolio alone cannot show: what it felt like to stand in front of the camera, how much help you received and whether the complete process matched what you had hoped for.

If we have recently worked together, I would be very grateful if you shared your experience. Your feedback genuinely matters and helps other people understand what they can expect.
