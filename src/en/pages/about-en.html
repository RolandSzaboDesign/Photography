Portraits showed me why I wanted to do this. lede: I began exploring photography more seriously in the summer of 2020 with my first compact camera. A few months later, in October, photographing an Audi RS5-R became my first client assignment. text1: At first, it was the technical side of automotive photography that interested me. I looked for the right light, location and viewpoint, then tried to show the car in the way its owner saw it. The assignment had a clear purpose, and the car was not nervous about being photographed. text2: In the summer of 2021, a friend invited me to a portrait session. He said it would be fun. At the time, I still felt awkward simply talking to a woman I had never met, let alone photographing her and giving her directions at the same time. text3: "As the session progressed, I could see the model becoming increasingly relaxed. After each important sequence, I showed her a few photographs on the camera, and her expression immediately showed that she liked what she saw. Later, I began hearing the same sentence from more and more people without modelling experience: “You’re the photographer. Tell me what to do.” That was when I understood that directing the session was my responsibility, and that the experience and photographs you take home depend greatly on me." takeaway: The technical side can be learned.
