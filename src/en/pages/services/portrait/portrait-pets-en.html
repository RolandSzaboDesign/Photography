I also need to know what excites them, what makes them uncertain and how easy it is to attract and maintain their attention. - A spacious park or urban location may work well with a calm, well-socialised dog. For a nervous, reactive or easily overwhelmed animal, we choose a quieter setting, a less busy time or a familiar indoor location. - A home or another familiar environment is often more comfortable for cats and other pets that do not enjoy travelling. In this case, we review the available light and useful spaces in advance. - title: A few familiar objects can make a significant difference paragraphs: - Bring water, treats, a favourite toy and the lead or harness your pet normally uses. It is better to work with equipment they already know and in which they can move safely. - The collar, harness and lead may be visible in several photographs. Clean and simple equipment generally attracts less attention, but it is not worth changing to something unfamiliar or uncomfortable purely for the photographs. - A short and calm walk before the session can help a dog settle, but there is no need to exhaust them completely. The series may include both active and quieter situations, so it helps if they retain some energy and interest. - title: Safety is more important than any visual idea paragraphs: - We only work without a lead in a place and situation where it is safe, permitted and your pet can be managed reliably. In every other situation, the lead or harness remains in use throughout the session. - It may be possible to remove the lead from certain photographs during editing, but this depends on the position, background and type of coat.
