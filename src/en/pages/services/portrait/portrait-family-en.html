It is generally easier to work with a well-rested child who has eaten than during nap time or at the end of a busy day when they are already tired and overstimulated. The time of day is therefore influenced by your family’s rhythm as well as by the light. - A location works well when it is safe, offers enough room to move and does not require the children to remain in one place throughout. A park or quieter outdoor setting is often more practical with younger children, while simpler and shorter setups may be more comfortable in a studio. - It is worth coordinating the clothes in colour and overall style, but everyone does not need to wear the same thing. Complementary colours and simpler patterns usually work well, while allowing each person to remain in clothes in which they can move comfortably. - A favourite toy, a small snack or another familiar object can also help. It is completely fine if the children first need to look around, move or focus on something else for a while.
