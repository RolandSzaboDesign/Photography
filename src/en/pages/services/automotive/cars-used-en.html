The model remains recognisable at a small size while the shape and overall condition of the bodywork are also visible. - This can be followed by an opposite three-quarter view, both side views and, when useful, a more direct front or rear photograph. The exact selection is adapted to the shape and condition of the vehicle. - Together, the first few photographs should show the complete exterior. It is not useful to fill the available positions with several very similar views of the same side while an important angle is missing completely. - title: The interior should show both use and equipment paragraphs: - We show the dashboard and front seats in a wider photograph that makes the layout and general condition of the interior clear. This can be supported by images of the rear seats and luggage compartment. - The instrument cluster, mileage, infotainment system, climate controls or another important feature may receive its own detail photograph. We primarily photograph elements that provide genuinely useful information to a potential buyer. - The luggage compartment may be more important for an estate, the additional row of seats for a seven-seater, the charging connection for an electric car and the roof condition for a convertible. The exact set of 12 photographs therefore differs slightly for every vehicle. - title: The photographs should be tidy but remain honest paragraphs: - It is worth showing the wheels, tyres, engine bay, valuable equipment and any damage or condition-related detail that a buyer should know about before arranging a viewing. - Editing brings consistency to the light, colours and appearance of the complete collection without changing the real condition of the vehicle.
